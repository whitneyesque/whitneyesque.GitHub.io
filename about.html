About

Good services don't come from one team. They come from getting the right people in the same room.

Most organizations aren't short on talent or good intentions. They're short on shared understanding. Teams build brilliant pieces that don't fit together. Users fall through the gaps between departments. The service, as a whole, was never designed.

I fix that.

I'm a service designer who works at the seam between strategy and execution. I run research that surfaces what's really happening, facilitate workshops that align people who've never been in the same room, and build artifacts (journey maps, blueprints, prototypes) that make the invisible visible. Then I stick around to help teams actually build the thing.

When I'm not mapping services, I'm probably baking fondant au chocolat, exploring San Francisco with my family, or reading about systems thinking. I believe the best design work happens when you genuinely care about the people inside the system you're trying to change.
