# Design-system-applier handoff — medical

## Layout archetype
12-section / rich (with restraint)

Reason: The case is research- and process-heavy — dual-track stakeholder + provider research, a three-revision synthesis arc, a workshop, and explicit reflection. The rich archetype's structural beats (StatRow → ProcessTimeline → InsightCallout → research findings → DarkBand → workshop → outcomes → reflection) match the prose's arc. Held to a single InsightCallout and a single DarkBand to keep visual restraint despite the section count.

## Components used (count 6 of 6 cap)
- **StatRow** — opening scope. Four stats from the polished draft (13 weeks, 10 interviews, 2 maps, 20 opportunities). Converted from the draft's `<StatRow><StatCard /></StatRow>` children pattern to the live site's `stats={[{ value, label }]}` array prop. `StatCard` doesn't exist as a component in the site repo.
- **InsightCallout** — placed at the top of the case after the business-stakes section. Carries the load-bearing "decision-making infrastructure for an org that did not yet have it" framing. Sage variant (default). Used once, never back-to-back with a second callout, per V4 restraint.
- **ProcessTimeline** — four-phase engagement structure (Stakeholder Research / Provider Research / Synthesis / Opportunity Workshop), placed at the start of the "Designing the research as two stages" section. Earns its place because the case argues the two-stage research design was deliberate; the timeline visually grounds the spine.
- **PullQuote** — three instances kept from the polished draft, no additions. (1) HME "social meter" line under the HME archetype; (2) Sleep specialist "patient blames me" line under the patient-in-the-gap section; (3) Whitney's reflection "research summary / design brief" inside the Synthesis section, intentionally without attribution to read as authorial framing rather than participant voice (matches the existing convention used in `ferguson-data.mdx`).
- **BotanicalDivider** — single instance at the narrative hinge between research findings ("the patient sits in the gap") and the design response (Synthesis as craft → Workshop). One-per-case rule observed.
- **DarkBand** — placed between the synthesis section and the workshop section, framing the two experience maps as the artifact that bridged synthesis and the room arguing over them. Two figure placeholders (HME map, sleep specialist map) — no h2 inside the band, since adding heading copy would require inventing words.

## Specimen choice
**Pomegranate (split)**

Reason: The polished draft's specimen line flagged this case as a "weave two distinct strands into one artifact" shape and proposed protea or pomegranate-split. The case's actual structural finding — *the patient sits in the gap between two halves of the same broken system* — maps more cleanly onto the pomegranate-split symbolism (revealing the system inside) than to the protea (many petals into one bloom). Pomegranate is also unused on the site (rose · agetech, ranunculus · ferguson-cx, protea · sfpl, cymbidium · ferguson-data), so this case gets its own motif. The pomegranate-split specimen is one of the two illustrative SVGs in the V4 mockup tab (Sample 1 / Minimal hero). If Whitney prefers protea, swap both `specimen` and `specimenSignoff` paths together — they're a pair.

(If you want a different specimen, change `specimen` and `specimenSignoff` in the MDX frontmatter.)

## Narrative content that didn't fit V4 vocabulary
- **`<RoleCard>` from the polished draft** — the live site has no RoleCard component. Converted the role detail into a regular `## Service designer and research lead.` h2 section followed by the role paragraph. The "My Role" label that the draft component carried is dropped; the heading itself names the role, and the case header already shows Role from frontmatter. No prose change.
- **`<StatCard>` children inside `<StatRow>`** — the live StatRow takes a `stats` array prop, not children. Converted four `<StatCard number="…" label="…" />` entries to `{ value, label }` objects. No prose change.
- **End-of-case `<SpecimenSignoff />`** — the V4 spec describes a sign-off ornament at the end of the case (the bottom watermark in both Sample 1 and Sample 2 mockups). The live site repo has no `SpecimenSignoff.astro` component (only the deprecation note is in `content/config.ts`, and no live case file uses it). Per the agent's hard rule ("If V4 has a component the live site doesn't yet have, note it in your handoff and skip it"), the case ends with the closing reflection and no signoff component. The `specimenSignoff` frontmatter field is preserved so the case is ready to opt in if a `SpecimenSignoff` component is added later.
- **Title typography** — the polished draft's title "Designing a Decision Tool for an Organization Learning to Decide Differently" is in Title Case with no terminal punctuation. The four shipped V4 cases (`agetech.mdx`, `ferguson-cx.mdx`, `sfpl.mdx`, `ferguson-data.mdx`) all use sentence case with terminal punctuation. Applied that convention here: rendered as `Designing a decision tool <em>for an organization learning to decide differently.</em>`. Word choice, sentence structure, and paragraph breaks are unchanged. If Whitney prefers Title Case, the swap is one line in frontmatter.
- **H2 terminal periods** — same convention applied to body h2s where the heading carries an `<em>` clause (matches the V4 mockup pattern: "## What we heard, *cropped from the affinity board.*"). No words added or rearranged; only the punctuation that closes the V4 italic phrase.
- **Hero image (`hero_image: '/images/cases/medical/hero.jpg'`)** — present in the polished draft but not referenced in the rendered hero (the site's `CaseLayout.astro` supports it but the four shipped V4 cases all rely on `specimen` only, and the path was a placeholder filename). Dropped from frontmatter. The specimen carries the visual hero load.
